## The Business Model

### PPP Concession Structure

```
BWSSB / Municipal Corp
        │
        │  Concession agreement (15 years)
        │  Tipping fee: ₹500–800/tonne of sludge processed
        │
        ▼
  GasChain CBG Unit
  (installed at STP site)
        │
        │  Purification → Compression → Bottling
        │
        ▼
  Commercial buyers
  Hospitals: ₹2,000/cylinder (19kg)
  Hotels: ₹2,000/cylinder
  Restaurant chains: ₹1,800/cylinder
```

### Revenue streams per STP unit

| Stream | Amount |
|---|---|
| Gas sales (commercial) | ₹1,800–2,000/cylinder |
| Tipping fee from BWSSB | ₹500–800/tonne sludge |
| Carbon credits (1T CBG = 2.9T CO₂) | ₹800–1,200/tonne CBG |
| Digestate (bio-fertiliser) | ₹2–4/kg |

### Why BWSSB will say yes

Currently BWSSB uses STP biogas to generate electricity at 30% efficiency.  
GasChain converts it to CBG at 90% efficiency and pays them a royalty.  
They get: more revenue per unit of biogas + tipping fee income + reduced sludge disposal cost.  
No capital expenditure from BWSSB required.

## Technical Specifications

### Conversion ratios
- 1 MLD of sewage → 20–25 m³ raw biogas/day
- 1 m³ raw biogas → ~0.5 kg CBG (after purification, 90% methane)
- 1 MLD → 10–12 kg CBG/day

### Quality standard
- IS 16087:2016 — CBG must contain >90% methane, <4% CO₂, <20 mg/m³ H₂S
- Sewage-based biogas requires additional H₂S scrubbing (higher concentration than agri-waste)
- Budget: ₹50–80 lakhs additional for robust H₂S removal unit

### Equipment required per 50 MLD STP unit
| Component | Cost estimate |
|---|---|
| Biogas collection cover (floating) | ₹15–25L |
| H₂S scrubber | ₹20–35L |
| CO₂ removal (membrane/PSA) | ₹35–60L |
| Compression unit (200 bar) | ₹25–40L |
| Cylinder filling station | ₹10–15L |
| Online gas analyser | ₹8–12L |
| **Total per unit** | **₹1.13–1.87 Cr** |

MNRE subsidy (50% for CBG plants): reduces to **₹57L–94L own capital** per unit.

## Go-to-market sequence

1. **Identify** — Map all BWSSB STPs with untapped biogas (use OpenCity data)
2. **Approach** — Write to BWSSB Chairman with PPP proposal (draft in docs/letters/)
3. **Pilot** — Sign concession for 1 STP (start with Vrishabhavathi — largest)
4. **Certify** — Get IS 16087:2016 certification for output gas
5. **Sell** — Sign 3 anchor commercial buyers (1 hospital, 1 hotel chain, 1 restaurant chain)
6. **Replicate** — Use pilot data to sign remaining Bengaluru STPs, then expand to other cities

## Competitors in this specific space

As of 2025, **no company** is operating an STP-based CBG commercial aggregator in Karnataka.

- VA Tech Wabag: builds STP infrastructure but does not bottle and sell CBG commercially
- Carbon Masters: food waste collector, no STP operations
- GPS Renewables: agri-waste and MSW focus, no STP presence in Karnataka
- EverEnviro: MSW focus, North India primarily

**The Karnataka STP-to-commercial-cylinder space is open.**
